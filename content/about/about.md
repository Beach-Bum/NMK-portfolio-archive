# Ned Karlovich

Creative Director. Brand Architect. Builder.

## About

I build brands at the edge of what technology makes possible.

For two decades I've led creative programs for companies that define culture — Nike, Adidas, Jordan Brand, Magic Leap, Givenchy, IKEA, Rimowa, Google — and for companies building the infrastructure of what comes next: Status.im, Logos.co, and my own ventures in AI and decentralized systems.

My work spans the full surface area of brand: visual identity systems, spatial and environmental design, product UX/UI, campaign direction, and the strategic frameworks that hold it all together. I've built brand guidelines that governed $30B+ product lines, designed retail environments on four continents, and led teams from 2-person startups to 200-person global creative departments.

What makes my background unusual is that I don't just design for technology — I build with it. I write code. I ship products. I architect systems. My current work includes an AI-powered trading platform (React/TypeScript, Python/FastAPI, deployed and live), a stablecoin payments app on Celo, and research into decentralized agent marketplace protocols using Google's A2A and Coinbase's x402. I think in systems, whether those systems are visual languages or software architectures.

I believe the most important design challenges of the next decade will happen at the intersection of AI and human experience — how autonomous systems communicate, how trust is established between agents and people, how interfaces adapt when the user might not be human. These are brand problems as much as they are engineering problems, and they require people who can think across both.
